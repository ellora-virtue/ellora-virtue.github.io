Learning Reflections

Friday 14th February 2020

My long term goal is to start a career in the technology sector. At this stage, I’m really interested in becoming a web developer (hence why I’m at EDA!), however I also have a strong interest in app development. This is something I might look at pursuing further down the line but for now, my main goal is to get a job as a junior developer and go from there.

In terms of what human skills I’d like to develop while at EDA, I’d really like to work on my time management skills. I’m a pretty organised person in general, but somehow I always find it difficult to organise my time efficiently and stay on-task.
Another human skill I’d like to work on is communication. This invaluable skill links closely with teamwork and leadership, both of which are crucial skills for working in tech. I do think I have pretty good communication skills already but I think there’s always room for improvement. From my understanding, there is a lot of teamwork involved in EDA so it’s very important that everybody communicates clearly and effectively.
Lastly, I would like to improve my active listening skills and take onboard other people’s ideas, feedback and opinions.

I believe my strengths currently lie in empathy, leadership and perfectionism. My limitations are also perfectionism (true double-edged sword!), self-doubt and saying “no”.

My commitment as to how I will manage my workload in the EDA programme is as follows:

I will organise my time effectively by mapping out when I will do work for EDA, when I have free time and when I am working at my other job (during Foundations). By doing this, I will ensure I have enough time to get through the content and extra time to revise/practice, as well as a bit of time to relax and do life admin without feeling guilty that I “should” be working. Obviously there will be a lot less opportunity for this “free time” once Bootcamp starts but I am aware of this and willing to commit myself and my time fully.
I will structure the time I am spending on EDA work so that I break down the week’s tasks into manageable chunks and make sure I am giving myself small breaks every 30 minutes to one hour (depending on how much of a roll I am on at that given moment).

A commitment as to how and where I will seek help in a timely way is as follows:

This is one of the big parts where my communication skills will come into play. I will communicate openly and honestly with my facilitator/s and other staff at EDA about how I am doing and what I might be struggling with. This will benefit both me, as I will feel comfortable that they are up to date on where I’m at, and the teachers/staff from EDA, as they will know how I’m finding the material and workload.
I hope that the facilitation team will be available for me to ask any questions I might have, give constructive feedback/criticism, let me know how I’m doing in the broader scheme of things and hopefully give some career/job seeking advice towards the end of EDA.

Scheduling information:

During Foundations, I am working 1-2 days a week in my old full-time job at a technology wholesaler. At the time of writing, this will fall on Wednesdays and sometimes Thursdays. Aside from this, I don’t have any other major commitments that I need to block-out time for.
